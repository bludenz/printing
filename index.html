Frequently Asked Questions

What is PLA?

PLA (Polylactic Acid) is the most popular and easiest filament to print with. It's biodegradable, made from renewable resources like corn starch, and produces prints with a smooth finish. It's great for beginners and general prototyping, but has lower heat resistance and durability compared to other plastics.

What is ABS used for?

ABS (Acrylonitrile Butadiene Styrene) is known for its strength, flexibility, and higher temperature resistance. It's commonly used for functional parts, tools, and objects that need to withstand wear and tear. However, it can be more challenging to print due to warping, and it emits fumes during printing that require good ventilation.

What is the difference between flexible filaments (TPU)?

Flexible filaments like TPU (Thermoplastic Polyurethane) are rubber-like plastics used for objects that need to bend, stretch, or absorb impact. They are excellent for phone cases, wearables, and gaskets. Printing with them requires careful calibration due to their elasticity, which can cause tangles if not handled correctly.

How do I prevent warping during printing?

Warping is common with materials like ABS and Nylon. To prevent it, ensure your print bed is level and properly adhered (using glue stick, hairspray, or special build surfaces). Use a heated print bed, keep your printer in an enclosure to maintain consistent temperature, and avoid drafts. Calibrate your print speed and cooling settings appropriately.
